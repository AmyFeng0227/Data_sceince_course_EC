    The Z-test and T-test are statistical methods used to determine whether there is a significant difference between the means of two groups, which may be related in certain features. The choice between a Z-test and a T-test generally depends on the sample size and whether the standard deviation of the population is known.

    Scipy doesn't have a dedicated function for the Z-test because it's generally used when you have a large sample size (typically n > 30) and know the population standard deviation. Under these conditions, the Central Limit Theorem states that the sampling distribution of the sample mean is normally distributed, and the Z-test becomes applicable.

    However, in real-world scenarios, the population standard deviation is rarely known, and the T-test, which uses the sample standard deviation as an estimate of the population standard deviation, becomes more appropriate. Scipy provides functions for the T-test (scipy.stats.ttest_ind for two independent samples, scipy.stats.ttest_rel for two related samples, and scipy.stats.ttest_1samp for a single sample compared to a known mean) because it's more commonly applicable.
